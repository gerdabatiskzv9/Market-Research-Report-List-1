<p><p>Aviation biofuel, also known as sustainable aviation fuel (SAF), is a type of biofuel specifically designed for use in aircraft. It is produced from renewable resources such as waste oils, agricultural residues, algae, and other non-fossil feedstocks. Unlike conventional jet fuel, aviation biofuel significantly reduces greenhouse gas emissions, making it a key component in the aviation industry's efforts to achieve sustainability and decarbonization goals.</p><p>The Aviation Biofuel Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.9% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing environmental regulations, rising demand for cleaner energy solutions, and the aviation industry's commitment to reducing its carbon footprint. Governments and airlines worldwide are investing in biofuel production and adoption, with initiatives like carbon offset programs and mandates for SAF blending in jet fuel.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include advancements in feedstock technology, such as the development of synthetic fuels and hydrogen-based alternatives, as well as partnerships between airlines, fuel producers, and research institutions. Additionally, the growing focus on circular economy practices is encouraging the use of waste-derived feedstocks for biofuel production. Despite challenges like high production costs and limited infrastructure, the aviation biofuel market is poised for significant expansion as the industry transitions toward sustainable aviation.</p></p>

<p><p>The aviation biofuel market includes types like Fischer-Tropsch (FT) and Hydrogenated Vegetable Oil (HVO). FT biofuels are produced through a chemical process converting biomass or waste into synthetic fuels, offering high energy density and compatibility with existing aircraft engines. HVO biofuels are derived from hydrogenating vegetable oils or animal fats, resulting in cleaner-burning, renewable jet fuel. Both types reduce carbon emissions and align with sustainability goals, with FT focusing on synthetic pathways and HVO on renewable feedstock processing for greener aviation solutions.</p></p>
