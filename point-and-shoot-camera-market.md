<p><ul><li>CCD Sensor</li><li>CMOS Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The point-and-shoot camera market primarily features two types of image sensors: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor). CCD sensors are known for their superior image quality and low noise levels, making them ideal for high-resolution photography under low light. Conversely, CMOS sensors are favored for their energy efficiency, rapid processing speeds, and ability to integrate additional functionalities on a single chip. This has led to their growing popularity in compact cameras, catering to varying consumer preferences and technological advancements.</p></p>
