<p><p>The tungsten ore market primarily revolves around three key minerals: wolframite, scheelite, and ferberite. Wolframite, a iron-manganese tungstate, is valued for its high tungsten content and is commonly used in industrial applications. Scheelite, a calcium tungstate, is prized for its purity and is often processed into tungsten powders. Ferberite, an iron-rich tungstate, is less common but still significant in tungsten production. These ores are essential for manufacturing hard metals, alloys, and electronics, with demand driven by aerospace, mining, and energy sectors. Market dynamics depend on supply, extraction costs, and industrial demand.</p></p>
