<p><p>The Mandibular Distraction Device market includes various types, with multi-vector distraction and bi-directional distraction being prominent. Multi-vector distraction allows for simultaneous bone growth in multiple directions, enhancing facial symmetry and alignment during treatment. In contrast, bi-directional distraction focuses on elongating the mandible in two opposing directions, typically beneficial for complex jaw deformities. Both methodologies aim to improve function and aesthetics, catering to diverse patient needs while advancing in technological applications and surgical techniques.</p></p>
