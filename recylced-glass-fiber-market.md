<p><p>The recycled glass fiber market primarily consists of two types: post-industrial glass fiber and recycled GFRP (Glass Fiber Reinforced Polymer). Post-industrial glass fiber is derived from manufacturing excess and waste, repurposing materials that would otherwise be discarded. On the other hand, recycled GFRP involves the reclamation and processing of end-of-life composite materials, allowing for the reuse of glass fibers in new applications. Both types contribute to sustainability and resource efficiency in various industries, including construction, automotive, and aerospace.</p></p>
