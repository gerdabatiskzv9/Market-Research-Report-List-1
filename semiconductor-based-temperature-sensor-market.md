<p><ul><li>Voltage Output Temperature Sensors</li><li>Current Output Temperature Sensors</li><li>Digital Output Temperature Sensors</li><li>Resistance Output Silicon Temperature Sensors</li><li>Diode Temperature Sensors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The semiconductor-based temperature sensor market includes various types tailored to specific applications. Voltage output temperature sensors generate a voltage proportional to temperature, while current output sensors produce a current signal. Digital output temperature sensors provide precise digital data for easy integration with microcontrollers. Resistance output silicon temperature sensors change resistance with temperature variations, ensuring accurate measurements. Diode temperature sensors utilize the voltage drop across a diode to determine temperature, offering simplicity and reliability in various environments.</p></p>
