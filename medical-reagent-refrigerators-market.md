<p><p>The medical reagent refrigerator market is divided into two main types: low-temperature refrigerators and ultra-low temperature refrigerators. Low-temperature refrigerators typically operate at temperatures between 2°C to 8°C, making them suitable for storing vaccines, pharmaceuticals, and biological samples. In contrast, ultra-low temperature refrigerators function at temperatures below -40°C, designed for long-term storage of sensitive materials like cryogenic samples and certain laboratory reagents. Both types are essential for maintaining the stability and efficacy of medical and biological products.</p></p>
