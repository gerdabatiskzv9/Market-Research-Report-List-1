Additionally, the shift toward autonomous and semi-autonomous vehicles is fueling demand for steer-by-wire systems, which eliminate mechanical linkages for enhanced flexibility and control.</p><p>Emerging trends include the adoption of lightweight materials to improve vehicle efficiency and the development of adaptive steering systems that adjust to driving conditions. <p><p>The Automotive Steering Market includes Electric Power Steering (EPS), Hydraulic Power Steering (HPS), and other systems. EPS uses an electric motor to assist steering, offering better fuel efficiency, precision, and adaptability. HPS relies on hydraulic pressure generated by a pump, providing robust performance but consuming more energy. Other systems may include electro-hydraulic or manual steering, catering to specific vehicle needs. EPS dominates due to its efficiency and integration with advanced driver-assistance systems, while HPS remains in heavy-duty vehicles for its durability.</p></p>
