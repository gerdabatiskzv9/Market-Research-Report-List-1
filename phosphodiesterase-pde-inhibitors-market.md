<p><p>Phosphodiesterase (PDE) inhibitors are a class of drugs that block the action of phosphodiesterase enzymes, which are responsible for breaking down cyclic nucleotides like cAMP and cGMP. By inhibiting these enzymes, PDE inhibitors enhance the effects of these signaling molecules, leading to various therapeutic benefits such as vasodilation, enhanced myocardial contractility, and increased intracellular signaling. These properties make PDE inhibitors valuable in treating conditions like erectile dysfunction, pulmonary hypertension, and certain heart diseases.</p><p>The PDE inhibitors market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.8% during the forecast period, driven by increasing prevalence of related health conditions, growing geriatric populations, and rising awareness of treatment options.
