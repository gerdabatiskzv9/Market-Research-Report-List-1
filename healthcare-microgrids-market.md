<p><p>Healthcare microgrids can be categorized into three main types: AC microgrid systems, which use alternating current for efficient energy distribution and are compatible with existing grid infrastructure; DC microgrid systems, which utilize direct current and are ideal for solar energy integration and battery storage; and hybrid microgrid systems, which combine both AC and DC components, allowing for enhanced flexibility and reliability in power management. These systems are crucial for ensuring uninterrupted power supply in healthcare facilities.</p></p>
